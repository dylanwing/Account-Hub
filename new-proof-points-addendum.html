Runway NEW
Generative AI / Video Generation
Runway built and launched Gen-3 Alpha — their most advanced video generation model — on Anyscale. The project required curating and processing massive quantities of video data (orders of magnitude larger than traditional structured data). Previously, researchers ran one-off scripts with manual dataset sharding, but Gen-3 Alpha demanded infrastructure that could scale. They used Ray for distributed computing ("It's hard to even compare Ray to anything — it's a bit of a default at this point") and Anyscale launched the Kubernetes Operator partnership with Runway to support their GKE-based infrastructure.
Gen-3 Alpha launched on Anyscale Massive video data preprocessing Ray Serve for last-mile preprocessing Anyscale Operator for Kubernetes
Source: Anyscale Case Study — Runway

Coinbase NEW
Fintech / ML Platform
Coinbase modernized their entire ML infrastructure on Ray and Anyscale. Before migration, training workloads couldn't scale, many jobs ran on single large-memory instances wasting resources, and HPO redundantly processed the same data. After migration: 15x more training jobs at the same cost, 50x larger datasets processed, training time reduced from 2 hours to seconds for some workloads. Ray's Python-native approach let teams work across data preprocessing, training, and deployment without switching tools.
15x more jobs at same cost 50x larger datasets processed 2 hours → seconds training time Python-native unified platform
Source: Anyscale Case Study — Coinbase

Amazon — Exabyte-Scale Migration NEW
Data Processing / Infrastructure
Amazon migrated their exabyte-scale data catalog management from Apache Spark to Ray — a 4-year journey. Ray achieved 82% better cost efficiency than Spark per GiB processed, translating to $120M/year in savings on EC2. The system processes 20+ PiB/day across 1600+ Ray jobs/day, with clusters containing up to 26,846 vCPUs and 210 TiB of RAM each. Ray maintained 100% on-time delivery rate throughout.
$120M/yr cost savings 82% more cost-efficient than Spark 20+ PiB/day processed 100% on-time delivery 1600+ Ray jobs/day
Source: AWS Blog — Amazon Spark to Ray Migration
